Understand what shortcuts are and which ones are the most important. Beginners guide for Shortcuts

Introduction

A keyboard shortcut is an action you would do with your mouse is mapped to a ceratin combination of keys. There are many of these so called keyboard shortcuts, some will make you more efficient, and others are really not worth mentioning.



WHAT YOU NEED

-Your Windows PC

Before we can start

When talking about shortcuts there are three different types of keys to talk about. There are the modifier keys, the standard keys and the function keys, each doing their own part in shortcuts

Modifier keys

The modifier keys are perhaps the most important keys, there is no shortcut without them. But what are modifier keys? They are the keys at the bottom left of your keyboard: ctrl, win and alt. And as their name suggests, they modify the behaviour of other keys when pressed in addition with them.


Regular Keys

These are quite simple, just the normal letters and numbers


Function Keys

The (most often tiny) keys at the very top of your keyboard. Depending on your keyboard they might also allow you to perform quick actions like turning the volume up or down when pressing the "fn" key.


One last thing

For some this might be painstakingly obvious, for others not so I'll mention it anyways: when performing a shortcut, the modifier key will have to be pressed first, similar to when capitalizing.


Copy, Cut and Paste

In my opinion an absolute must-have, but a shocking amount of people still do these with a right-click. If you are writing a fair amount of stuff, then adapting these will make you many times more efficient.

Copy = ctrl + c

Cut = ctrl + x

Paste = ctrl + v



My Favorite: Clipboard Memory

To this day one of my favorite shortcuts, the clipboard memory. You cut one thing you really wanted to keep and then you copied something because you forgot about it, with clipboard history this won't be a problem anymore. After pressing the shortcut for the very first time you will have to activate it. After this you will be able to pull up a menu of all the things you copied in this session. Just simply press on the item and it will get inserted.

Clipboard Memory = win + v


Select All

Want to select a whole document, this shortcut got you covered

Select All = ctrl + a


Force Close a program and / or shutdown your computer

Perhaps one of my most used shortcuts, I don't close a program any other way anymore, nor do I shutdown my computer any way. This Shortcut will close the active program and if no program is active or all windows are closed, you will get prompted with a Shutdown Window. If you want to shut down your PC, just press enter once that window gets shown.

Force Close and Shutdown = alt + f4


Undo, Redo

Deleted something you didn't intend, or the opposite: you made too much undo. These shortcuts have you covered, especially since there are programs where this functionality is only available via shortcuts

Undo = ctrl + z

Redo = ctrl + y



Delete Whole Words

Made a typo and you're much faster rewriting the word or words, with this shortcut you can delete whole words at a time so you will be much quicker

Delete Whole Words = ctrl + backspace


Lock PC

Leaving your PC unattended for just a moment can lead to some major inconvinience, especially if you are a teacher or work with childern in another way. To avoid these inconviniences just lock your PC, opon returning you will just have to enter your pin and everything will be there again.

Lock PC = win + L


Make a Screenshot

If you want to show somebody what's on your screen or you want to save what your seeing, just make a screenshot, after pressing the shortcut you will be able to select which part of your screen should be saved, once selected a notification will pop up which will give you the option to save or share it.

Screenshot = win + shift + s


Minimize All

If somebody is talking to you at your desk and they should not see or are not allowed to see what you are working on, just quickly minimize all your windows with win + d, after you are done just press it again and all windows will appear again.

Minimize All = win + D


Minimize Only Briefly

Should you only want to minimize everything for a very short moment, for example if somebody is just walking behind you and they shouldn't become too curious, just press win + , and your windows will dissapear only as long as you leave the keys pressed down and will instantly pop up again if you release them agian.

Minimize Temporarily = win + ,


Switch Between Windows

If you are often switching between two or more windows, for example because you have to type out something, use the alt + tab shortcut to quickly switch beween your windows, if pressed you will switch between your current and the last used window. Should you want to switch between three or more windows, leave alt pressed down, and press tab again, you will be able to select throug a miniaturized version of your windows.

Switch Windows= alt + tab
